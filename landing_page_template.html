The Ligandable Human Proteome

  • Protein domains are ranked based on their recurrence in human proteins (column #4)
  • Ligandability: the median desolvation of ligands co-crystallized with these domains (column #5) and, when available, their median affinity (column #6), are indicative of the domain’s ligandability
  • Chemistry: to view 2D structures of ligands, click numbers under “Druglike ligands in PDB” (column #7)
  • Binding pockets: to interactively view 3D structures of binding pockets, click numbers under “Structures with bound ligand in the PDB” (column #8), and click a PDB code
  • Chemical coverage: “Proteins with bound ligands in the PDB” (column #9) is indicative of the chemical coverage of this domain
  • Structural coverage: “Proteins with structures in the PDB with or without ligand” (column #10) is indicative of the structural coverage of this domain

Median ligand desolvation (%)
For each 3D structure of a drug-like ligand bound to the specified domain of a human protein, the % of the ligand surface that becomes shielded from solvent upon binding is calculated. If multiple structures are available for a given protein, the highest value is retained. The percentages shown in this column are the median values across all proteins for the specified domain. The higher the number, the more desolvated the compound, the more enclosed - and presumably ligandable - the pocket. This value is less reliable when it is derived from few proteins (column “Proteins with bound ligand in the PDB”)
> 80%: ligandable.
< 70% not ligandable
Median affinity (nM)
For each 3D structure of a drug-like ligand bound to the specified domain of a human protein, the affinity of the ligand for the protein domain is extracted from the PDBbind database (http://www.pdbbind.org.cn/). If multiple structures are available for a given protein, the lowest value (i.e. highest affinity) is retained. The values shown in this column are the median values across all proteins for the specified domain. The lower the number, the more ligandable the pocket. This value is less reliable when it is derived from few proteins (column “Proteins with ligand affinity data”)
